Who qualifies for STEM OPT and what are the rules?

F-1 students with a qualifying STEM degree can extend their standard 12-month OPT by 24 months, for a total of 36 months. The employer must be enrolled in E-Verify, and you must have a formal training plan (Form I-983) in place. Apply before your current OPT expires.

Applies to: F-1 Verified April 21, 2026

Who this applies to

STEM OPT is available to F-1 students who have: (1) received a degree in a qualifying STEM field, (2) are currently on a valid 12-month OPT EAD issued for work related to that STEM degree, and (3) are employed (or have an offer) with a U.S. employer enrolled in E-Verify.

What the law says

Under 8 CFR § 214.2(f)(10)(ii)(C), a 24-month extension of OPT is available for eligible students. The combined standard OPT plus STEM OPT extension totals 36 months of OPT work authorization.

Qualifying STEM degree:
Your degree must be in a field listed on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List. The list includes fields like computer science, engineering, mathematics, statistics, biological sciences, chemistry, and many others. The degree used must be within the past 10 years and from a SEVP-certified school.

E-Verify employer requirement:
Your employer must be enrolled in the E-Verify program at the time you apply and throughout your STEM OPT employment. Self-employment and employment through staffing agencies that place you with a third-party employer present complications — the actual worksite employer (not just the staffing agency) must be the E-Verify-enrolled entity.

Training Plan (Form I-983):
Before applying, you and your employer must complete Form I-983 — a formal training plan that details the learning objectives, supervision structure, and goals of the training period. The form must be signed by you and an authorized representative of your employer, then reviewed and approved by your DSO.

How to apply:

  1. Obtain DSO recommendation on a new I-20 endorsing STEM OPT extension
  2. File Form I-765 with USCIS before your current OPT EAD expires (applying at least 90 days before expiration is strongly recommended)
  3. Receive new EAD with the extended dates

Ongoing reporting requirements:
During STEM OPT you must report to your DSO every 6 months to confirm your employment status. You must also report any changes in employment, including employer changes, layoffs, or changes in job duties that no longer align with the training plan.

Common gotchas

  • Apply before your current OPT expires. If your OPT EAD expires before USCIS processes the extension, you must stop working. USCIS may grant a 180-day automatic extension of work authorization if you apply on time, but this is only available if the application was timely filed.
  • Self-employment is not permitted. Unlike some EAD categories, STEM OPT does not allow self-employment. You must work for a qualifying E-Verify employer.
  • Staffing agency arrangements require careful analysis. If a staffing agency places you at a client company, the client company (the actual worksite employer) must be E-Verify enrolled, and the training relationship must be with the client, not just the agency.
  • The DHS STEM list is updated periodically. A field that was on the list when you enrolled may not be on it when you graduate, or vice versa. Check the current list.
  • Changing employers requires a new I-983 and DSO update. You must update SEVIS through your DSO within 10 days of changing employers.
  • The 60-day unemployment allowance. During the STEM OPT extension period, you have 60 days of authorized unemployment (in addition to the 90 days during standard OPT). These do not combine — the 60-day clock runs separately during the extension period.
  • Using a prior degree: You may use a prior qualifying STEM degree (even if your current OPT was authorized based on a non-STEM degree) if the STEM degree is from a SEVP-certified school and is within the past 10 years. Confirm with your DSO.

What this page does not cover

  • Standard OPT — the 12-month period that precedes STEM OPT
  • The 90-day unemployment rule — applies during standard OPT; the STEM OPT extension has its own 60-day allowance
  • CPT — curriculum-linked training
