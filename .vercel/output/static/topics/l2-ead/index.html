Can L-2 spouses work?

Yes. As of January 30, 2023, L-2 spouses are automatically authorized to work in the United States incident to their L-2 status—no separate EAD is required to have work authorization. However, obtaining an EAD (Form I-766) is recommended for ease of completing the Form I-9 with employers.

Applies to: L-2 Verified April 21, 2026

Who this applies to

This page applies to spouses of L-1 visa holders (L-1A and L-1B) who hold L-2 status. L-2 children (unmarried children under 21) do not have work authorization. This page describes the current rule effective January 30, 2023.

What the law says

Following the settlement in Shergill v. Mayorkas (N.D. Cal. 2021) and the subsequent DHS final rule effective January 30, 2023, L-2 spouses are authorized to work incident to their status under 8 CFR § 274a.12(c)(18).

What “incident to status” means:
Work authorization flows automatically from holding valid L-2 status. You do not need to apply for a separate EAD to have work authorization. Your L-2 I-94 (the arrival/departure record, available at CBP’s I-94 website) serves as evidence of your authorized period of stay.

Why you may still want an EAD:
Many employers are not familiar with the incident-to-status rule and may be unsure how to complete the I-9 using only an I-94. Obtaining an EAD (Form I-766) provides a single, unambiguous document that employers recognize for I-9 purposes. The EAD is optional but practically useful.

How to get an EAD (if desired):
File Form I-765 with USCIS, using category code (c)(18). Include:

  • Copy of your I-94 showing L-2 status
  • Copy of the L-1 principal’s I-94 or visa evidence
  • Applicable fees and supporting documentation

Duration:
L-2 work authorization is valid for as long as you maintain valid L-2 status. If the L-1 principal’s status changes, extends, or lapses, your L-2 status (and work authorization) is affected.

Common gotchas

  • Your work authorization is incident to L-2 status — not the L-2 visa stamp. The visa stamp is used to enter the United States; your I-94 shows your authorized period of stay. Work authorization ends when your authorized period of stay (as shown on I-94) ends, even if a visa stamp in your passport still shows a future expiration date.
  • L-2 children cannot work. The incident-to-status work authorization rule applies only to spouses. Children in L-2 status have no work authorization.
  • This rule was not always the law. Before the 2023 regulation, L-2 spouses needed an EAD and could not work without one. If you or an employer is operating under outdated information, the new rule controls.
  • I-9 documentation: Because many employers are unfamiliar with L-2 incident-to-status work authorization, some may incorrectly demand an EAD. Under the I-9 rules, you cannot be required to produce a specific document — you may use any List A, B, or C combination that proves identity and work authorization. Your I-94 (List C) plus a List B document (ID) can satisfy the I-9. However, having an EAD simplifies this considerably.
  • Stay on top of status validity. If the L-1 principal’s I-94 or petition expires, your L-2 status — and work authorization — may lapse. Coordinate renewals with the L-1 principal’s employer.
