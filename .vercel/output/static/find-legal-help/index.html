Find Legal Help

The resources below are free or low-cost. This list is not an endorsement, and we are not affiliated with any of these organizations. Always verify an attorney's credentials before sharing personal information.

National nonprofits

  • Immigration Legal Resource Center — immigrationlawhelp.org

    A searchable directory of free and low-cost immigration legal service providers across the United States, operated by the Immigration Advocates Network.

    Legal directory

  • Catholic Legal Immigration Network (CLINIC)

    One of the largest networks of nonprofit immigration legal service providers in the country, with more than 400 affiliate programs in 49 states.

    National nonprofit network

  • AILA National Pro Bono Program

    The American Immigration Lawyers Association coordinates pro bono immigration legal representation. Use their directory to find pro bono clinics and participating attorneys.

    Pro bono referrals

  • National Immigration Law Center (NILC)

    Focuses on advocacy and litigation for low-income immigrants, and provides referrals to legal service organizations.

    Advocacy / referrals

Law school clinics

Law school immigration clinics provide free representation under the supervision of licensed attorneys. Availability and case types vary by school.

  • Yale Law School — Jerome N. Frank Legal Services Organization

    Provides free legal services in immigration and other matters to low-income clients in Connecticut.

    Law school clinic

  • Harvard Law School — Immigration and Refugee Clinical Program

    Represents asylum seekers, refugees, and undocumented individuals in removal proceedings and related matters.

    Law school clinic

  • Georgetown University Law Center — Immigration Clinic

    Represents low-income immigrants in the DC metropolitan area in removal defense, asylum, and relief from removal.

    Law school clinic

  • NYU School of Law — Immigrant Rights Clinic

    Provides direct representation and engages in policy advocacy on behalf of immigrants in New York.

    Law school clinic

  • University of Chicago Law School — Immigration Clinic

    Provides free legal representation to immigrants in Chicago and surrounding areas.

    Law school clinic

Verify someone's credentials

Only licensed attorneys and DOJ-accredited representatives are authorized to provide immigration legal services for a fee. You can verify whether someone is on the DOJ EOIR accredited representative roster using the link above.

To verify an attorney's license, look up your state bar association's attorney directory. Most state bars have online lookup tools.

Red flags: notario fraud and immigration scams

Immigration fraud — including "notario fraud" — is a serious problem. A notario (or "notario público") in many Latin American countries is a licensed legal professional, but in the United States the title has no legal meaning and does not qualify someone to provide immigration legal advice.

Warning signs of a scam:

  • Promises to "fix" your immigration status quickly or guarantees a specific outcome
  • Asks you to sign blank forms
  • Refuses to give you copies of documents filed on your behalf
  • Claims to have special connections at USCIS or an immigration court
  • Is not a licensed attorney or DOJ-accredited representative but still charges for immigration help
  • Uses titles like "notario," "notario público," "immigration consultant," or "visa consultant" and charges for legal advice

Resources: FTC — Immigration Services Fraud • USCIS — Avoid Scams
