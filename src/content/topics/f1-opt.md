## Who this applies to

OPT is available to F-1 students who have been enrolled full time for at least one full academic year. It covers off-campus employment that is directly related to the student's major field of study. Most students use post-completion OPT — the 12-month period after graduation — but pre-completion OPT is also possible.

## What the law says

Under [8 CFR § 214.2(f)(10)(ii)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-8/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-214/section-214.2), OPT is divided into two types:

**Pre-completion OPT** — taken before the student completes their program:
- May be part-time (20 hours or fewer per week) while school is in session
- May be full-time during school breaks or after completing all coursework but before the program end date
- Any pre-completion OPT used is deducted from the 12-month total

**Post-completion OPT** — taken after the student finishes the program:
- May be full-time
- Must be directly related to the major field of study
- The standard period is 12 months

**How to apply:**

1. Request a DSO recommendation in SEVIS. Your DSO will issue a new I-20 recommending OPT.
2. File Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization) with USCIS within 30 days of the DSO's recommendation date.
3. USCIS issues an Employment Authorization Document (EAD, Form I-766).
4. **You may not work until you receive the physical EAD card and your start date arrives** — even if you know the application is approved.

**Timing rules:**
- You may apply up to 90 days before your program end date (for post-completion OPT)
- Your OPT start date cannot be more than 60 days after your program end date
- You must have your EAD in hand before you begin working

## Common gotchas

- **You need the physical EAD card.** Approval notices and USCIS online status updates do not authorize work. Wait for the card.
- **The 90-day unemployment rule.** During OPT, you may be unemployed for a maximum of 90 days cumulative. Exceeding this violates your status. See the [90-day unemployment rule page](/topics/f1-90-day-rule) for details.
- **Part-time employment counts against the 90-day rule differently.** Working fewer than 20 hours per week during post-completion OPT is considered unemployment unless you are in a degree program part time.
- **The work must relate to your major.** "Related to your field of study" is broadly interpreted, but there should be a genuine connection. Completely unrelated work (e.g., a computer science graduate doing restaurant work full time) does not count as authorized OPT employment.
- **You cannot begin new full-time CPT after OPT authorization begins.** OPT and CPT are separate programs, but the 12-month OPT clock limits your options.
- **Applying late.** If you miss the 30-day window after DSO recommendation or the 90-day pre-graduation window, you may lose your OPT eligibility for that program. Timing is critical.
- **STEM OPT extension must be applied for before OPT expires.** See the [STEM OPT page](/topics/f1-stem-opt).
