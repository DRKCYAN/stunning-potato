## Who this applies to

This page applies to students maintaining valid F-1 nonimmigrant status at a Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP)-certified school. It covers only **on-campus employment** — work performed on the school's premises or at an off-campus location that is educationally affiliated with the school. Separate rules govern Curricular Practical Training (CPT), Optional Practical Training (OPT), and off-campus work due to severe economic hardship.

## What the law says

Under [8 CFR § 214.2(f)(9)(i)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-8/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-214/section-214.2), an F-1 student is authorized to work on campus without any special USCIS filing or DSO authorization, subject to three core conditions:

1. **The student must be maintaining valid F-1 status** — enrolled full time and complying with all conditions of that status.
2. **While school is in session**, the student may not work more than **20 hours per week**.
3. **During official school vacations** (summer, winter break, spring break) and after completing a full course of study but before the program end date, the student may work **full time** (more than 20 hours per week).

"On-campus" means either:
- Work performed on the **school's physical premises**, or
- Work at an **off-campus location that is educationally affiliated** with the school — for example, a research station or a company that has a contract with the school to provide services to students on campus (such as a food service company operating the school cafeteria).

The employer must be either the school itself or an on-campus commercial firm that directly serves students. Working for a company that merely has an office on campus but does not serve the student body is **not** considered on-campus employment.

## Common gotchas

- **The 20-hour limit is strict.** Exceeding it — even by a few hours — violates your F-1 status. This can trigger removal proceedings and affect future visa applications.
- **Two on-campus jobs? Still 20 hours total.** If you hold more than one on-campus job, the 20-hour limit applies to your combined hours across all positions.
- **Off-campus contractors do qualify, but narrowly.** Not every company with a campus presence counts. The contractor must provide services that primarily serve the students of the institution (e.g., a bookstore operator, dining hall vendor, or campus health clinic).
- **You don't report on-campus work to USCIS or your DSO.** Unlike CPT, on-campus employment does not require any special authorization or SEVIS update. Your I-20 and enrollment status are sufficient.
- **Losing full-time status suspends the privilege.** If you drop below full-time enrollment without an approved exception, you are no longer in valid F-1 status and on-campus work authorization ceases.
- **Work authorization does not extend during a grace period.** After your program end date, you are in a 60-day grace period (or 15 days for certain completions), but you are not authorized to continue working.

## What this page does not cover

- [Curricular Practical Training (CPT)](/topics/f1-cpt) — off-campus work that is a required or integral part of your curriculum
- [Optional Practical Training (OPT)](/topics/f1-opt) — post-completion or pre-completion off-campus training
- [STEM OPT extension](/topics/f1-stem-opt) — the 24-month extension for STEM degree holders
- Off-campus work due to severe economic hardship (requires separate USCIS approval via Form I-765)
- J-1 on-campus employment (different rules apply)

## If you need legal help

If you have questions about whether a specific position qualifies as on-campus employment, start with your school's **Designated School Official (DSO)** — they are the first resource for F-1 employment questions. If you need legal advice beyond what the DSO can provide:

- [immigrationlawhelp.org](https://immigrationlawhelp.org) — searchable directory of free and low-cost legal services
- [CLINIC](https://www.clinic.org) — network of nonprofit immigration legal service providers
- [AILA National Pro Bono](https://www.aila.org/practice/pro-bono) — pro bono immigration legal representation
