## Who this applies to

This page applies to **spouses** holding H-4 status whose H-1B principal (spouse) meets one of two qualifying criteria. H-4 **children** are not eligible for work authorization. H-4 status is derivative — it depends on the H-1B principal's status remaining valid.

## What the law says

Under [8 CFR § 274a.12(c)(26)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-8/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-274a/section-274a.12), an H-4 spouse is eligible to apply for an EAD if the H-1B principal has **either**:

**Qualifying condition 1:** An approved Form I-140 (Immigrant Petition for Alien Workers) that has not been revoked. This means the H-1B holder has already reached at least the I-140 approval stage of the employment-based green card process.

**Qualifying condition 2:** Has been granted an extension of H-1B status beyond the standard 6-year cap limit. This happens under the American Competitiveness in the Twenty-first Century Act (AC21), which allows H-1B extensions in one-year or three-year increments when a green card application has been pending for a sufficient period.

**How to apply:**  
File Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization) with USCIS, using category code **(c)(26)**. You must submit supporting evidence including:
- Evidence of your own H-4 status (copy of I-94, visa stamp)
- Evidence that the H-1B principal meets a qualifying condition (copy of I-140 approval notice, or evidence of AC21 extension)
- Form I-539 (if applying to change/extend H-4 status concurrently, or if filing without already being in H-4 status)

**The EAD is not employer-specific.** Unlike H-1B authorization, an H-4 EAD allows you to work for any employer, in any occupation, anywhere in the United States — similar to an unrestricted EAD.

**Duration:** The H-4 EAD is typically issued to expire on the same date as the H-4 I-94 (or the H-1B principal's authorized stay). Renewals must be filed before the EAD expires.

## Common gotchas

- **Do not work before receiving the EAD.** Even if you are clearly eligible, your H-4 status does not authorize work. You must have the physical EAD card in hand and your start date must have arrived before starting work.
- **The EAD is tied to your H-4 status.** If the H-1B principal loses their H-1B status, your H-4 status is affected, and your EAD may no longer be valid. Monitor your H-4 status carefully.
- **Pending I-140 is not enough.** Only an *approved* I-140 qualifies. A pending I-140 application does not make you eligible.
- **H-4 EAD policy has been subject to legal challenges.** The H-4 EAD rule has faced litigation at various points. Check [USCIS's current guidance](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/employment-authorization-for-certain-h-4-dependent-spouses) for the current status of the program.
- **Processing times can be long.** File as early as possible. Premium processing is not available for Form I-765.
- **Renewing on time is critical.** Premium processing for H-1B extensions (which would reset the H-4 validity period) does not automatically extend your EAD. File your EAD renewal application well before expiration.
- **Children in H-4 status cannot work, even if a parent has an H-4 EAD.** The EAD eligibility is limited to spouses.
